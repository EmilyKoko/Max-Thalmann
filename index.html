Max Thalmann (1890-1944)

After completing an apprenticeship in bookbinding, Thalmann studied at the School of Applied Arts in Weimar. In 1919, he served as a master at the Bauhaus Weimar in art and design.

He was most prolific in the 20s and 30s, producing expressionist architectural and figurative woodcuts.

Outside of Germany, a large collection of his work was held by his brother, who had emigrated to America. This collection has been preserved in Los Angeles at Loyola Marymount University and UCLA.
